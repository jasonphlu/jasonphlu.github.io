Research Interest

Optimization and machine learning to innovate transportation systems with the goal to make more intelligent and operationally efficient mobility, transit, and freight systems.

Undergraduate Research Experience

I am an Undergraduate Research Assistant in the Socially Aware Mobility Lab under Prof. Pascal Van Hentenryck. The core focus of the lab is on On-Demand Multimodal Transit Systems: a transit system made up of fixed bus and rail networks with shuttles routed dynamically based on transit demand. ODMTS innovates transit by solving the first/last mile problem and providing convenience to riders. This video goes into ODMTS in more detail! I work on a series of projects contributing to ODMTS.

Working Papers

Jason Lu, Anthony Trasatti, Hongzhao Guan, Kevin Dalmeijer, Pascal Van Hentenryck, "Impacts of Dedicated Bus Lanes and Congestion on On-Demand Multimodal Transit Systems"

This project was the first project I lead from beginning to end, culminating with my first lead-author paper.

Ongoing Projects

Optimization of Network Design in ODMTS

The optimization of network design for ODMTS is always advancing. In this project, the network design advances by adding a formulation to model bus lines in the system in order to more accurately represent wait times in transit. I contributed to this project by implementing methods to account for objective costs among riders through different bus lines. I also created data for the case study and ran some initial experiments on the path evaluation implementations for riders.

MARTA Reach

Marta Reach was a six-month pilot of the ODMTS applied to MARTA. You can take a closer look at the MARTA Reach Pilot here!

In the Pilot project, I am investigating the effects of shuttle fleet sizing and autonomous vehicles in the Pilot.

Demand Mode Choice Modeling in ODMTS

The goal for this project is to find a better way to model adoption and mode choice among transit riders in ODMTS. I'm studying mode choice modeling and multinominal logistic regression.
